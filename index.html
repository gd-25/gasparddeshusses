Born in March 1991, Gaspard Deshusses is an independant French artist and graphic designer who likes to shred fashion magazines.

Probably nothing makes him feel more electrified than tearing up a blood red scrap of paper off of a glossy page. Having been raised between the city and the mountains, he then lived in New York City, Paris and the French Alps for several years. Gaspard was constantly surrounded around graphic lines. The wild and brutal lines of ridges and peaks, in contrast to the neat and geometric lines of buildings and avenues made him who he is. Now, with stolen ripped off shapes and colors, he aims to create wild and sensual moods through methodic and structured compositions. This orderly chaos that Gaspard designs, is his chosen way to illustrate a paradox between a form of contrast, of confrontation– and eventually, an inevitable form of harmony and coexistence.

Dazzled by the techniques and aesthetics of abstract expressionism, Gaspard has been particularly influenced by the work of artists Nicolas de Staël and Jean-Michel Basquiat.
His process as well as the collages that he creates are fundamentally experimental, which is the reason why most them are named with numbers instead of actual titles– this helps him focus on a kind of obsessive, endless visual research, through a consistent and profuse production.

This website showcased his artworks randomly and not chronologically. You can keep up with his latest work on his personal Instagram account.

EDUCATION

2014 / 2016 – MFA Visual Communication - Creapole ESDI, Paris, France

2011 / 2014 – BFA Visual Communication - Creapole ESDI, Paris, France

CONTRIBUTION

April 2020 – upcoming project for WWD magazine, digital publication worldwide

July 2016 – showing of ten framed collages for a collective exhibition in Bozel, France
